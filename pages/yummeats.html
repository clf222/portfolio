Case Studies

YummEats

Summary

Mealsharing is one of the newer innovations of the sharing economy. It allows home-based chefs the opportunity to run a business from their own kitchen, on their own terms. As an industry, it is developing on several distinct paths. 1. Destination Dining. Championed by Vizeat, diners book immersive experiences outside of their home area. More often than not, experiences are booked in a foreign country. 2. Event Dining. Championed by Feastly, diners book events either in a chef's home or in a space managed by the company. A third branch has begun to take hold; targeting the every-day consumer, take-out meal sharing lets consumers purchase innovatative take-out directly from skilled chefs in their own area. Josephine is making in-roads in this market on the West Coast.

The sharing economy is still emerging. “The sharing economy is estimated to grow from $14 billion in 2014 to $335 billion by 2025. [1]” The digital technology that created platforms to get a cheaper ride or a place to stay is ready to be adopted by other segments of the economy. In 2016, 52% of Americans ordered take-out from several times a month or more [2]. A robust meal-sharing application is the ideal platform to bring busy consumers a variety of cuisines prepared by home-based chefs. This is the market segment that YummEats will fill.

[1]“The Current and Future State of the Sharing Economy.” Niam Yaraghi and Shamika Ravi.

[2]Frequency of ordering food for takeout in the U.S. 2016

Problem

The problem presented is to create an app that appears professional, trustworthy and inviting to a market that is mostly unfamiliar with the concept of mealsharing. The potential user needs to be able to find purchasing opportunities near either where they are currently or where they are going to be.

Balancing the need for a professional appearance with an inviting emotional call to action will be critical to the application's success.
